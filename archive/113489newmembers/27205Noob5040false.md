#### [ Mario Carneiro (Oct 30 2018 at 18:23)](https://leanprover.zulipchat.com/#narrow/stream/113489-new%20members/topic/Noob%3A%2050%20%3D%2040%20-%3E%20false/near/136792029):
<p>I think <code>dec_trivial</code> has a clean explanation in the type theory. Roughly, there is a term of type <code>nat -&gt; nat -&gt; bool</code> that says whether two natural numbers are equal or not, and we assert that this is <code>tt</code> by refl, so in order for the <code>dec_trivial</code> term to typecheck it forces computation of this function. The function itself is easy to define by recursion on the natural numbers.</p>
